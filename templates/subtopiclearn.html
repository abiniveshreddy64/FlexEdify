Brief Steps to solve:

Step 1: Write down the quadratic equation in the form (ax^2 + bx + c = 0), where a, b are coefficients and c is constant.
Step 2: For the equation (y^2 + 10y + 21 = 0), the coefficients are (a = 1), (b = 10), and (c = 21).
Step 3: Calculate the discriminant using the formula (D = b^2 - 4ac).
Step 4: For this equation, (D = 10^2 - 4(1)(21)).
Step 5: Evaluate the discriminant: (D > 0), so there are two real and distinct roots.
Step 6: Use the quadratic formula to find the roots: (y = (-b +- (D)**0.5)/2a).
Step 7: Substitute the values of (a), (b), and (D) into the formula to find the roots.
Step 8: Simplify the expressions to get the numerical values of the roots.
